Understanding adjusting entries: the backbone of accurate financials

GM Gurjot Mann, CPA  ·  Jun 12, 2026  ·  6 min read
AE

Adjusting entries are the quiet step between a messy trial balance and financial statements you can actually trust. They rarely get attention, but they are where the accrual basis of accounting really happens. Get them right and your numbers tell the truth. Skip them and every ratio, every comparison, and every decision built on those statements is a little bit off.

What an adjusting entry actually does

At its core, an adjusting entry moves revenue and expenses into the period they belong to, regardless of when cash changed hands. The cash side has already been recorded, or will be, but the income statement needs to reflect what was earned and incurred during the period. That is the whole job.

There are four classic types worth knowing cold:

  • Accrued revenue — earned but not yet billed or received.
  • Accrued expenses — incurred but not yet paid, such as wages or interest at period end.
  • Deferred revenue — cash received before the work is done.
  • Prepaid expenses — cash paid before the benefit is used, like insurance or rent.
If cash and the income statement disagree about timing, an adjusting entry is usually the fix.

Why they matter more than they look

A company can be cash-positive and still be losing money, or cash-poor and quietly profitable. Adjusting entries are what surface that reality. Depreciation spreads the cost of an asset across the years it is used. An accrual for unpaid wages keeps December's labour in December. Without these, comparability breaks down and trends become noise.

A simple period-end routine

  1. Start from a reconciled trial balance. Adjusting entries on top of unreconciled balances just move the error around.
  2. Walk the balance sheet line by line and ask, "what does this number assume, and is that still true?"
  3. Document the support for every entry. A future reviewer, including future you, should be able to rebuild it.
  4. Re-run your statements and sanity-check the movement, not just the ending balance.

The mindset

Treat adjusting entries as the place where judgment lives, not as mechanical clean-up. The double rule under a final total means the figure is balanced and final. Adjusting entries are how you earn the right to draw it.
