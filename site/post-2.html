Year-end tax tips every Canadian small business should know

GM Gurjot Mann, CPA  ·  Jun 10, 2026  ·  6 min read
AE

Year-end is not the time to discover surprises. The owners who pay the least and stress the least are the ones who did a little housekeeping before the clock ran out. None of this is exotic, it is just a handful of moves that consistently pay off.

1. Clean the books before you plan

Tax planning on top of messy records is guesswork. Reconcile your bank and credit card accounts, clear the suspense balances, and make sure every owner draw is coded correctly. A clean trial balance is the foundation everything else sits on.

2. Time income and expenses with intent

If you are near a tax bracket threshold, the timing of an invoice or a purchase can matter. Where it makes business sense, consider accelerating a needed expense into this year or deferring discretionary income. The key phrase is "where it makes business sense", never let the tax tail wag the dog.

3. Revisit how you pay yourself

Salary versus dividends is not a one-size answer. It affects RRSP room, CPP, and the overall integration of corporate and personal tax. Run the numbers for your actual situation each year, the right mix can shift as rates and your income change.

The goal is not to pay zero tax. It is to pay the right amount, on purpose, with no penalties.

4. Don't leave credits on the table

  • Capital cost allowance on equipment placed in service this year.
  • Input tax credits on GST/HST you actually paid.
  • Home-office and vehicle costs, properly supported.

5. Get your slips and deadlines mapped

T4s and T5s, instalment due dates, and the corporate filing deadline all have a way of arriving at once. Put them on a calendar now so nothing turns into a late-filing penalty later.

None of this replaces advice for your specific situation, and I am not your accountant through a blog post. But a business owner who walks into year-end with clean books and a short checklist is already ahead of most.
