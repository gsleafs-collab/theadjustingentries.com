A CaseWare workflow that saves hours on every file

GM Gurjot Mann, CPA  ·  May 28, 2026  ·  5 min read
AE

Most of the time we lose on an engagement is not in the hard judgment calls, it is in the setup, the re-keying, and the second-guessing about whether a number tied out last year. A few habits in CaseWare and CaseView remove that drag and let you spend your time where it actually matters.

1. Template the file you wish you had

Build a master template with your standard lead schedules, mapping, and document structure already in place. Starting every engagement from a clean, consistent skeleton means less time wiring up the file and more time on the work.

2. Map once, roll forward forever

Invest in a clean chart-of-accounts mapping the first year. When you roll forward, the structure, comparatives, and most of the formatting come with you. A disciplined roll forward is the single biggest time saver in the tool.

If you are reformatting statements by hand every year, the file is working for you instead of the other way around.

3. Let CaseView do the math

Link your statements and notes to the trial balance rather than typing figures. When an adjusting entry lands, everything downstream updates, and you remove an entire category of "the note does not agree to the face" errors.

4. Build review into the file, not after it

  • Use tickmarks and sign-offs as you go, not in a panic at the end.
  • Keep a short open-items list inside the file so nothing lives only in your head.
  • Document the "why," not just the "what," so the reviewer is not chasing you.

None of these are clever tricks. They are just small, repeatable habits that compound. Do them on every file and the hours you save add up to entire days over a season.
